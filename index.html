The Sonnet’s Argument
This lesson invites a slow reading of Shakespeare’s sonnets, focusing on how thought unfolds across fourteen lines. Rather than extracting themes, we attend to movement: shifts in tone, turns in reasoning, and the pressure language exerts on feeling.
Encounter
Read Sonnet 18

Shall I compare thee to a summer’s day…

Notice
Where does the argument turn?

Track tonal movement. Where does admiration become assertion?

Argue
What is the poem trying to secure?

Is immortality emotional, rhetorical, or poetic?

Write
Form a claim

Construct an interpretation grounded in structure, not theme summary.
