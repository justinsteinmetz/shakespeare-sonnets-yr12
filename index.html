Shakespeare's Sonnets 18 & 130 — rhetoric, form, and the problem of love

I · Opening 3 min

What is a love poem for?

Is a love poem meant to elevate, preserve, persuade — or to tell the truth? Shakespeare’s sonnets test each possibility, often in tension with one another.

II · Form 5 min

The sonnet as argument

A Shakespearean sonnet unfolds as a structured act of reasoning. Each quatrain develops pressure; the volta reorients the logic; the couplet resolves — or complicates — the claim.
